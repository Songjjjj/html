Key Findings

Income Correlation

For every $10,000 increase in average neighborhood income, there is an average increase of 3.2 gyms per square mile.

Price Segmentation

High-income areas (>$100k) show 5x higher average membership costs compared to lower-income neighborhoods.

Facility Types

Luxury and boutique gyms concentrate in areas with average incomes above $100,000, while community centers dominate in areas below $50,000.

Accessibility

Lower-income neighborhoods show a 60% lower gym-to-population ratio compared to higher-income areas.
